Solvation Thermodynamics in BEA and FAU Zeolites

DFT Molecular Dynamics Multiscale Modeling Zeolite Catalysis Solvation Thermodynamics

Overview

Many liquid-phase reactions in chemical manufacturing are carried out inside zeolites — crystalline, microporous aluminosilicates whose nanometer-scale pores confine both reactants and solvent. Inside these pores, solvent molecules behave very differently from how they do in bulk liquid, and this confined solvation environment can strongly influence reaction rates and selectivity. This project develops a quantitative understanding of solvation thermodynamics in two industrially important zeolite frameworks, BEA and FAU, and how the local solvent environment shapes the energetics of adsorbed species.

Why It Matters

Capturing solvation effects is essential for predictive catalyst design, yet it is challenging because the relevant physics spans multiple length and time scales. BEA and FAU differ in pore geometry and connectivity, which changes how solvent organizes around an adsorbate:

  • BEA — a three-dimensional, intersecting channel system with relatively confined pores.
  • FAU — large supercages connected by smaller windows, giving a more open environment.
  • Comparing the two frameworks isolates how confinement and pore topology govern solvation.

Approach: Multiscale Modeling

No single method captures both the accuracy of bond-level interactions and the statistics of many solvent molecules. The work therefore combines methods across scales:

  • Density Functional Theory (DFT) — accurate adsorbate–framework interactions and reaction energetics at active sites.
  • Molecular Dynamics (MD) — sampling of solvent configurations and dynamics inside the pores to capture entropy and ensemble averages.
  • Free-energy / solvation analysis — extracting solvation free energies and thermodynamic contributions from the simulations.
  • Multiple adsorbates — studying a range of probe molecules to map how chemistry and size affect solvation across both frameworks.

Current Status

  • Building and validating BEA and FAU framework models with adsorbates and explicit solvent.
  • Running DFT and MD calculations to quantify solvation free energies across systems.
